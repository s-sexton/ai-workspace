# SendThisFile Marketing Agent Roadmap

## Mission

The SendThisFile Marketing Agent helps SendThisFile grow qualified traffic,
trial starts, and revenue by analyzing approved marketing data, identifying
high-leverage opportunities, and recommending low-cost actions.

It does not replace human judgment. It recommends, explains, prioritizes, and
prepares work for approval.

## Operating Principles

- Prefer low-cost, compounding channels before paid acquisition.
- Measure every recommendation against business outcomes, not vanity metrics.
- Use local analysis whenever practical.
- Keep credentials, analytics exports, and customer data out of source control.
- Treat Google Analytics, Search Console, CRM, billing, and website data as
  sensitive business information.
- Make bold recommendations, but clearly label facts, assumptions, risks, and
  experiments.
- Avoid autonomous publishing, ad spend, vendor purchases, or site changes
  without explicit approval.

## Primary Outcomes

The Marketing Agent should optimize for:

- More qualified organic traffic.
- More high-intent visitors reaching conversion pages.
- Higher visitor-to-trial or visitor-to-lead conversion.
- Better understanding of which channels produce retained customers.
- Lower cost per qualified opportunity.
- Stronger SendThisFile positioning against generic file-transfer tools.

## Current Assumptions

These assumptions should be validated with analytics and business data:

- Budget is low, so organic search, conversion optimization, partner/referral
  loops, and repurposed content should come before paid campaigns.
- SendThisFile likely has stronger purchase intent around secure file transfer,
  business file sharing, compliance-sensitive transfer, and alternatives to
  consumer file-sharing tools.
- Existing site traffic may contain underused intent signals in landing pages,
  support pages, branded search, legacy backlinks, and product comparison
  queries.
- Google Analytics alone is not enough; Search Console and conversion data are
  needed to connect traffic to business value.

## Data Sources

Initial approved sources:

- Google Analytics traffic, events, conversions, landing pages, source/medium,
  campaigns, geography, device, and funnel behavior.
- Google Search Console queries, impressions, clicks, click-through rate,
  ranking position, indexed pages, and technical issues.
- Website crawl data for titles, headings, meta descriptions, canonical tags,
  internal links, broken pages, redirects, and page speed signals.
- Existing website content, pricing pages, signup flows, product pages, support
  content, and comparison pages.
- CRM, billing, or trial data only when explicitly approved and scoped.

Future approved sources:

- Email campaign performance.
- Customer support themes.
- Win/loss notes.
- Competitor positioning snapshots.
- Backlink and referral data.

## Hard Boundaries

The Marketing Agent must not act without explicit approval when an action would:

- Change website content or metadata.
- Publish blog posts, pages, ads, emails, or social posts.
- Spend money.
- Modify tracking, analytics, tags, consent, or authentication settings.
- Export or transmit proprietary data to third-party tools.
- Contact customers, prospects, partners, journalists, or vendors.

## Phase 1: Measurement Foundation

Status: starting.

Goal: know what is working before recommending major effort.

Deliverables:

- Define the core marketing scorecard:
  - Sessions by channel.
  - Organic clicks and impressions.
  - Top landing pages.
  - Conversion rate by landing page and channel.
  - Trial, lead, signup, or contact events.
  - Assisted conversions where available.
  - Branded vs non-branded search.
- Audit Google Analytics setup:
  - Confirm key events and conversions.
  - Identify missing events in the signup/contact funnel.
  - Check referral exclusions and self-referrals.
  - Check source/medium cleanliness.
  - Flag pages with traffic but no measurable next action.
- Audit Search Console setup:
  - Find high-impression, low-click queries.
  - Find pages ranking positions 4-20.
  - Find pages losing clicks.
  - Find crawl/index coverage issues.
- Produce a local Markdown baseline report.

First bold recommendation:

If conversion tracking is incomplete, pause all discretionary marketing work
until the top conversion events and landing-page attribution are trustworthy.
Low-budget marketing cannot afford blind effort.

## Phase 2: Fast SEO Wins

Status: next.

Goal: increase qualified organic traffic with changes that do not require a
large content program.

Deliverables:

- Identify pages ranking positions 4-20 and improve them first.
- Rewrite weak titles and meta descriptions for high-impression pages.
- Strengthen internal links to pages with business intent.
- Build or improve pages around high-intent terms:
  - secure file transfer for business
  - encrypted file transfer
  - large file transfer for businesses
  - secure document delivery
  - file transfer for accountants
  - file transfer for legal teams
  - HIPAA-sensitive file transfer, if legally accurate
  - alternatives to consumer file-sharing tools
- Create comparison and problem pages before generic blog posts.
- Refresh or consolidate thin, duplicate, or outdated pages.
- Fix technical SEO issues that block crawling, indexing, speed, or mobile use.

First bold recommendation:

Do not start with a broad blog calendar. Start with commercial-intent pages,
comparison pages, and existing pages already near page one. That is where low
budget has the best odds of compounding.

## Phase 3: Conversion And Messaging

Status: planned.

Goal: turn more existing traffic into qualified action.

Deliverables:

- Review the homepage, pricing page, signup path, contact path, and top organic
  landing pages.
- Identify unclear positioning, weak calls to action, trust gaps, and friction.
- Map landing pages to visitor intent:
  - urgent transfer need
  - compliance/security concern
  - business process need
  - comparison shopping
  - existing customer support
- Recommend page-specific calls to action.
- Add trust signals where supported:
  - security posture
  - business use cases
  - file size or workflow limits
  - customer proof
  - retention and delivery controls
  - support reliability
- Create a prioritized test backlog with effort, expected impact, and risk.

First bold recommendation:

If SendThisFile has strong security or business reliability proof, make it
visible earlier. Buyers of secure transfer tools are often not looking for
clever copy; they are looking for confidence.

## Phase 4: Low-Cost Traffic Loops

Status: planned.

Goal: build repeatable traffic sources that do not depend on ongoing ad spend.

Candidate initiatives:

- SEO refresh loop:
  - Monthly Search Console analysis.
  - Update pages with rising impressions and weak click-through rate.
  - Strengthen internal links to pages near page one.
- Use-case page loop:
  - Build pages for accountant, legal, healthcare-adjacent, HR, finance, and IT
    workflows where accurate.
  - Connect each page to a specific conversion path.
- Comparison loop:
  - Create factual alternative pages for generic tools and risky workflows.
  - Avoid unsupported claims.
- Support-to-SEO loop:
  - Convert repeated support questions into durable help pages.
  - Link relevant help pages to product pages where appropriate.
- Partner/referral loop:
  - Identify professional service firms, IT consultants, MSPs, accounting
    groups, and compliance-adjacent communities.
  - Offer co-branded guides, not expensive sponsorships first.
- Email reactivation loop:
  - Segment dormant trials or inactive accounts only after approval.
  - Prepare useful, non-spammy reactivation campaigns.

First bold recommendation:

Favor niche professional workflows over generic "send large files" content.
Generic terms are expensive and crowded. Workflow-specific pages can attract
smaller but more qualified traffic.

## Phase 5: Competitive Positioning

Status: planned.

Goal: make SendThisFile easier to choose.

Deliverables:

- Identify top competitor categories:
  - consumer cloud storage
  - enterprise file-sharing suites
  - ad hoc transfer tools
  - secure email portals
  - industry-specific portals
- Build a positioning matrix:
  - security
  - ease of use
  - auditability
  - file size
  - recipient experience
  - administration
  - price/value
- Recommend website messaging changes.
- Prepare comparison-page outlines with claims that require verification.

First bold recommendation:

Do not compete as "another file transfer tool." Compete as the trusted business
handoff for files that should not be sent casually.

## Phase 6: Experiment System

Status: planned.

Goal: make marketing repeatable, measurable, and cheap to learn from.

Deliverables:

- Maintain a local experiment backlog:
  - hypothesis
  - audience
  - page/channel
  - expected business impact
  - cost
  - effort
  - risk
  - measurement plan
  - decision date
- Score initiatives with an impact/confidence/effort model.
- Produce weekly or monthly marketing briefs.
- Track outcomes against baseline.
- Recommend stop, continue, or double-down decisions.

Experiment priority order:

1. Fix measurement gaps.
2. Improve high-intent pages already receiving traffic.
3. Improve conversion paths.
4. Build focused landing pages.
5. Run small distribution tests.
6. Consider paid tests only when tracking and landing pages are ready.

## First 30 Days

Week 1:

- Confirm approved access/export process for Google Analytics and Search
  Console.
- Define the marketing scorecard.
- Produce the baseline analytics report.
- Identify top 10 landing pages by traffic and top 10 by conversion value.

Week 2:

- Complete Search Console opportunity analysis.
- Identify pages ranking positions 4-20.
- Create a title/meta/internal-link improvement backlog.
- Find high-impression, low-click pages.

Week 3:

- Review top conversion pages and signup/contact paths.
- Create a conversion friction report.
- Recommend 5-10 low-effort page improvements.
- Draft the first two high-intent landing page outlines.

Week 4:

- Deliver the first monthly marketing roadmap review.
- Select 3 approved experiments for the next month.
- Define exact success metrics for each experiment.
- Prepare implementation briefs for approved website/content changes.

## 90-Day Target State

By day 90, the Marketing Agent should support:

- A repeatable local marketing scorecard.
- Monthly SEO opportunity briefs.
- A prioritized website improvement backlog.
- A conversion friction backlog.
- A set of approved high-intent landing page briefs.
- A documented experiment system.
- Clear recommendations for what to stop, start, and double down on.

## Initial KPI Targets

These are starting targets and should be revised after baseline data:

- Increase non-branded organic clicks by 10-20% within 90 days.
- Improve click-through rate on selected high-impression pages by 15%.
- Move at least 5 target queries from positions 4-20 into the top 3-10.
- Improve conversion rate on top landing pages by 5-15%.
- Identify at least 3 low-cost partner or referral opportunities.

## Immediate Questions To Answer

- What are the top traffic sources by qualified conversion, not just sessions?
- Which landing pages attract visitors but fail to move them forward?
- Which non-branded queries already show purchase intent?
- Which pages are ranking close to page one?
- Are conversions and events trustworthy in Google Analytics?
- Which customer segments produce the best retention or revenue?
- What claims can SendThisFile safely make about security, compliance, and
  reliability?
